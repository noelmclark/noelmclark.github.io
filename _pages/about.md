I am currently an PhD student in the Fish & Wildlife Biology Program at the University of Montana in Missoula where I am leading a manipulative experiment to test the effects of wood addition on salmonid populations in northern Japan. This work is funded by an NSF Graduate Reserach Fellowship and in collaboration with the Tomakomai Experimental Forest, Hokkaido University. 

In 2025, I finished my M.S. at Colorado State University in Fort Collins where I studied conservation genomics of Colorado's Cutthroat Trout (O. clarkii spp​). I received a B.S. in marine science with a minor in statistics from Cal Poly in San Luis Obispo in 2021. As a hard worker fueled by an immense curiosity to learn, I am rarely pulled away from a project which grabs my attention.

In August 2022, I finished a position with NOAA Fisheries as a corpsmember with the Watershed Stewards Program, California Conservation Corps in partnership with Americorps. This position gave me invaluable, hands-on experience with collaborative fisheries resource management.

Besides wrangling fish and conducting research, you can find me exploring local trails on feet and wheels, climbing, writing, reading, and drinking coffee. I have published peer reviewed papers, informal articles, and even poetry! I am also involved in science communication and community outreach, and love getting involved in local projects.
